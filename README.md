# Customer Retention & Churn Analysis

---

## Problem Statement
Customer churn significantly impacts subscription-based businesses by increasing acquisition costs and reducing long-term profitability. 
Understanding churn patterns is essential for designing effective retention strategies.

---

## Project Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze customer data to identify churn patterns, key retention drivers, and customer lifetime trends. 
The goal is to generate actionable insights that can help reduce churn and improve customer retention.

---

## Dataset Description
The dataset contains customer-level subscription data from a telecom company. Each record represents an individual customer and includes demographic details, service information, contract type, payment method, monthly charges, tenure, and churn status.

---

## Key Insights
- Customers on month-to-month contracts exhibit significantly higher churn rates.
- Short-tenure customers are more likely to churn.
- Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn probability.
- Certain payment methods show elevated churn behavior.
- Long-term contract customers demonstrate strong retention patterns.

---

## Strategic Recommendations
- Encourage migration to long-term contracts through loyalty discounts.
- Implement targeted retention campaigns during the first 6 months.
- Reassess pricing strategy for high-risk segments.
- Provide personalized engagement for customers with high churn probability.

---

## Conclusion
The analysis identifies key behavioral, contractual, and pricing factors influencing customer churn. 
Proactive retention strategies targeting high-risk segments can significantly reduce customer attrition and improve overall business sustainability.
